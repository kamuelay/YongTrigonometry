Note that since \(OP\text{,}\) \(OQ\text{,}\) \(OA\text{,}\) and \(OB\) are lines from the center to points on the unit circle, they are congruent and have length of 1. Also note that \(\angle POB=\angle AOB=\alpha-\beta\text{.}\) Then since two sides and the included angle of \(\Delta OPQ\) and \(\Delta OAB\) are equivalent, we can conclude by side-angle-side theorem (SAS) that the two triangles are congruent. Thus, \(d(PQ)=d(AB)\text{.}\) Substituting our results for \(d(P,Q)\) and \(d(A,B)\) we get

\begin{align*} d(PQ)\amp =\amp d(AB)\\ \sqrt{2-2\cos\alpha\cos\beta-2\sin\alpha\sin\beta}\amp =\amp \sqrt{2-2\cos(\alpha-\beta)}\\ 2-2\cos\alpha\cos\beta-2\sin\alpha\sin\beta\amp =\amp 2-2\cos(\alpha-\beta)\\ -2\cos\alpha\cos\beta-2\sin\alpha\sin\beta\amp =\amp -2\cos(\alpha-\beta) \end{align*}
