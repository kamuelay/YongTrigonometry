Similarly, we run into problems for \(\csc\theta\) and \(\cot\theta\) when \(y=0\) since we would divide by 0. This corresponds to when the terminal side of \(\theta\) lies on the \(x\)-axis. These points on the unit circle are \((1,0)\) and \((-1,0)\) and have corresponding angles \(0\) and \(\pi\text{,}\) as well as all their coterminal angles: \(\ldots, -2\pi, -\pi, 0, \pi, 2\pi, \ldots\) (\(\ldots, -360^{\circ}, -180^{\circ}, 0^{\circ}, 180^{\circ}, 360^{\circ}, \ldots\)). In general, any angle of the form \(\pi\cdot n\) (\(180^{\circ}\cdot n\)) for some integer \(n\) should be excluded from the domain.
