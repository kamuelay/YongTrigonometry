Subsection 3.2.1 Sum and Difference Formulas

\begin{mybox}[Sum and Difference Formulas for Cosine]

\begin{align*} \cos(\alpha+\beta)\amp =\amp \cos\alpha\cos\beta-\sin\alpha\sin\beta\\ \cos(\alpha-\beta)\amp =\amp \cos\alpha\cos\beta+\sin\alpha\sin\beta \end{align*}

\end{mybox}

Proof.

First we will prove the Difference Formula for Cosine

\begin{equation*} \cos(\alpha-\beta)=\cos\alpha\cos\beta+\sin\alpha\sin\beta \end{equation*}

We begin by considering two points on the unit circle. Point \(P\) is at an angle of \(\beta\) in standard position with coordinates \((\cos\beta,\sin\beta)\) and point \(Q\) is at an angle of \(\alpha\) in standard position with coordinates \((\cos\alpha,\sin\alpha)\text{.}\)

We use the Distance Formula to calculate the distance between \(P\) and \(Q\) to get

\begin{align*} d(P,Q)\amp =\amp \sqrt{(\cos\alpha-\cos\beta)^2+(\sin\alpha-\sin\beta)^2}\\ \amp =\amp \sqrt{\cos^2\alpha-2\cos\alpha\cos\beta+\cos^2\beta+\sin^2\alpha-2\sin\alpha\sin\beta+\sin^2\beta}\\ \amp =\amp \sqrt{(\cos^2\alpha+\sin^2\alpha)+(\cos^2\beta+\sin^2\beta)-2\cos\alpha\cos\beta-2\sin\alpha\sin\beta} \end{align*}

Then by the Pythagorean Identify,

\begin{align*} d(P,Q)\amp =\amp \sqrt{1+1-2\cos\alpha\cos\beta-2\sin\alpha\sin\beta}\\ \amp =\amp \sqrt{2-2\cos\alpha\cos\beta-2\sin\alpha\sin\beta} \end{align*}

Next, consider two additional points on a second unit circle. Point \(A\) has coordinates at \((1,0)\) and Point \(B\) is at an angle of \(\alpha-\beta\) in standard position with coordinates \((\cos(\alpha-\beta),\sin(\alpha-\beta))\text{.}\)

The distance between \(A\) and \(B\) is

\begin{align*} d(A,B)\amp =\amp \sqrt{(\cos(\alpha-\beta)-1)^2+(\sin(\alpha-\beta)-0)^2}\\ \amp =\amp \sqrt{\cos^2(\alpha-\beta)-2\cos(\alpha-\beta)+1+\sin^2(\alpha-\beta)}\\ \amp =\amp \sqrt{(\cos^2(\alpha-\beta)+\sin^2(\alpha-\beta))-2\cos(\alpha-\beta)+1}\\ \amp =\amp \sqrt{1-2\cos(\alpha-\beta)+1}\\ \amp =\amp \sqrt{2-2\cos(\alpha-\beta)} \end{align*}

Note that since \(OP\text{,}\) \(OQ\text{,}\) \(OA\text{,}\) and \(OB\) are lines from the center to points on the unit circle, they are congruent and have length of 1. Also note that \(\angle POB=\angle AOB=\alpha-\beta\text{.}\) Then since two sides and the included angle of \(\Delta OPQ\) and \(\Delta OAB\) are equivalent, we can conclude by side-angle-side theorem (SAS) that the two triangles are congruent. Thus, \(d(PQ)=d(AB)\text{.}\) Substituting our results for \(d(P,Q)\) and \(d(A,B)\) we get

\begin{align*} d(PQ)\amp =\amp d(AB)\\ \sqrt{2-2\cos\alpha\cos\beta-2\sin\alpha\sin\beta}\amp =\amp \sqrt{2-2\cos(\alpha-\beta)}\\ 2-2\cos\alpha\cos\beta-2\sin\alpha\sin\beta\amp =\amp 2-2\cos(\alpha-\beta)\\ -2\cos\alpha\cos\beta-2\sin\alpha\sin\beta\amp =\amp -2\cos(\alpha-\beta) \end{align*}

Dividing both sides by -2 we arrive at the Difference Formula for Cosine

\begin{equation*} \cos\alpha\cos\beta+\sin\alpha\sin\beta=\cos(\alpha-\beta) \end{equation*}

To prove the Sum Formula for Cosine, replace \(\beta\) with \(-\beta\) in the Difference Formula and use the Even-Odd Identities where \(\sin(-\beta)=-\sin\beta\) and \(\cos(-\beta)=\cos\beta\) to get

\begin{align*} \cos\alpha\cos(-\beta)+\sin\alpha\sin(-\beta)\amp =\amp \cos(\alpha-(-\beta))\\ \cos\alpha\cos(\beta)+\sin\alpha(-\sin\beta)\amp =\amp \cos(\alpha+\beta)\\ \cos\alpha\cos\beta-\sin\alpha\sin\beta\amp =\amp \cos(\alpha+\beta) \end{align*}

Example 3.2.1.

Find the exact value of \(\cos105^{\circ}\text{.}\)

Solution.

First note that \(105^{\circ}=60^{\circ}+45^{\circ}\text{.}\) Now, using the Sum Formula for Cosine,

\begin{align*} \cos105^{\circ}\amp =\amp \cos(60^{\circ}+45^{\circ})\\ \amp =\amp \cos60^{\circ}\cos45^{\circ}-\sin60^{\circ}\sin45^{\circ}\\ \amp =\amp \frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\\ \amp =\amp \frac{1}{4}\left(\sqrt{2}-\sqrt{6}\right) \end{align*}

Example 3.2.2.

Find the exact value of \(\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{\pi}{6}\right)\text{.}\)

Solution.

Using the Difference Formula for Cosine we get

\begin{align*} \cos\left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{\pi}{6}\right)\amp =\amp \cos\frac{\pi}{4}\cos\frac{\pi}{6}+\sin\frac{\pi}{4}\sin\frac{\pi}{6}\\ \amp =\amp \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\cdot\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}+\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\cdot\frac{1}{2}\\ \amp =\amp \frac{1}{4}\left(\sqrt{6}+\sqrt{2}\right) \end{align*}

Example 3.2.3.

Find the exact value of the expression \(\cos25^{\circ}\cos35^{\circ}-\sin25^{\circ}\sin35^{\circ}\text{.}\)

Solution.

Notice this expression is the Sum Formula for Cosine with \(\alpha=25^{\circ}\) and \(\beta=35^{\circ}\text{.}\) So

\begin{equation*} \cos25^{\circ}\cos35^{\circ}-\sin25^{\circ}\sin35^{\circ}=\cos(25^{\circ}+35^{\circ})=\cos60^{\circ}=\frac{1}{2} \end{equation*}

\begin{mybox}[Sum and Difference Formulas for Sine]

\begin{align*} \sin(\alpha+\beta)\amp =\amp \sin\alpha\cos\beta+\cos\alpha\sin\beta\\ \sin(\alpha-\beta)\amp =\amp \sin\alpha\cos\beta-\cos\alpha\sin\beta \end{align*}

\end{mybox} We will prove the Sum Formula for Sine in Example 3.2.7 and the Difference Formula can be established using the Even-Odd Identities.

Example 3.2.4.

Use the Difference Formula for Sine to establish the identity \(\cos\theta=\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right)\)

Solution.

To establish an identity, we will start from one side of the equality and use properties to end up with the expression on the other side of the equality. So,

\begin{equation*} \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right)=\sin\frac{\pi}{2}\cos\theta-\cos\frac{\pi}{2}\sin\theta=1\cdot\cos\theta-0\cdot\sin\theta=\cos\theta \end{equation*}

Visually, we have \(\cos\theta=\frac{x}{r}=\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right)\text{:}\)

Example 3.2.5.

Given \(\sin\alpha=-\frac{12}{13}\text{,}\) with \(\frac{3\pi}{2}\lt \alpha\lt 2\pi\) and \(\cos\beta=-\frac{3}{5}\text{,}\) with \(\frac{\pi}{2}\lt \beta\lt \pi\text{,}\) find the exact value of \(\sin(\alpha+\beta)\text{.}\)

Solution.

The Sum Formula for Sine gives us

\begin{equation*} \sin(\alpha+\beta)=\sin\alpha\cos\beta+\cos\alpha\sin\beta \end{equation*}

At this moment, we do not know the exact values of \(\cos\alpha\) and \(\sin\beta\) but we can compute them.

Given \(\sin\alpha=-\frac{12}{13}\) with \(\frac{3\pi}{2}\lt \alpha\lt 2\pi\) and \(\cos\beta=-\frac{3}{5}\) with \(\frac{\pi}{2}\lt \beta\lt \pi\text{,}\) we can draw the following triangles associated with \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\text{,}\) respectively:

Next, using the Pythagorean Theorem, we solve for the missing sides on the triangles

\begin{align*} x^2+(-12)^2\amp =\amp 13^2\\ x+144\amp =\amp 169\\ x^2\amp =\amp 25\\ x\amp =\amp 5 \end{align*}

Thus we get

\begin{equation*} \cos\alpha=\frac{5}{13} \end{equation*}
\begin{align*} (-3)^2+y^2\amp =\amp 5^2\\ 9+y^2\amp =\amp 25\\ y^2\amp =\amp 16\\ y\amp =\amp 4 \end{align*}

Thus we get

\begin{equation*} \sin\beta=-\frac{4}{5} \end{equation*}

We now have all the information needed to proceed.

\begin{align*} \sin(\alpha+\beta)\amp =\amp \sin\alpha\cos\beta+\cos\alpha\sin\beta\\ \amp =\amp \left(-\frac{12}{13}\right)\left(-\frac{3}{5}\right)+\left(\frac{5}{13}\right)\left(\frac{4}{5}\right)\\ \amp =\amp \frac{36}{65}+\frac{20}{65}\\ \amp =\amp \frac{56}{65} \end{align*}

\begin{mybox}[Sum and Difference Formulas for Tangent]

\begin{align*} \tan(\alpha+\beta)\amp =\amp \frac{\tan\alpha+\tan\beta}{1-\tan\alpha\tan\beta}\\ \tan(\alpha-\beta)\amp =\amp \frac{\tan\alpha-\tan\beta}{1-\tan\alpha\tan\beta} \end{align*}

\end{mybox}

Proof.

Recall that \(\tan\theta=\frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}\) as long as \(\cos\theta\neq0\text{.}\) Using this fact, and our new formulas for the sum of sine and cosine, we get

\begin{align*} \tan(\alpha+\beta)\amp =\amp \frac{\sin(\alpha+\beta)}{\cos(\alpha+\beta)}\\ \amp =\amp \frac{\sin\alpha\cos\beta+\cos\alpha\sin\beta}{\cos\alpha\cos\beta-\sin\alpha\sin\beta}\\ \amp =\amp \frac{\dfrac{\sin\alpha\cos\beta+\cos\alpha\sin\beta}{\cos\alpha\cos\beta}}{\dfrac{\cos\alpha\cos\beta-\sin\alpha\sin\beta}{\cos\alpha\cos\beta}}\\ \amp =\amp \frac{\sin\alpha\cos\beta+\cos\alpha\sin\beta}{\cancel{\cos\alpha\cos\beta}}\cdot\frac{\cancel{\cos\alpha\cos\beta}}{\cos\alpha\cos\beta-\sin\alpha\sin\beta}\\ \amp =\amp \frac{\dfrac{\sin\alpha\cancel{\cos\beta}}{\cos\alpha\cancel{\cos\beta}}+\dfrac{\cancel{\cos\alpha}\sin\beta}{\cancel{\cos\alpha}\cos\beta}}{\dfrac{\cancel{\cos\alpha\cos\beta}}{\cancel{\cos\alpha\cos\beta}}-\dfrac{\sin\alpha\sin\beta}{\cos\alpha\cos\beta}}\\ \amp =\amp \frac{\dfrac{\sin\alpha}{\cos\alpha}+\dfrac{\sin\beta}{\cos\beta}}{1-\dfrac{\sin\alpha}{\cos\alpha}\dfrac{\sin\beta}{\cos\beta}}\\ \amp =\amp \frac{\tan\alpha+\tan\beta}{1-\tan\alpha\tan\beta} \end{align*}

The difference formula can be established using the Even-Odd Identities.

Example 3.2.6.

Find the exact value of \(\tan\left(\frac{3\pi}{4}+\frac{\pi}{6}\right)\)

Solution.

Using the sum formula for tangent, we get

\begin{equation*} \tan\left(\frac{3\pi}{4}+\frac{\pi}{6}\right)=\frac{\tan\frac{3\pi}{4}+\tan\frac{\pi}{6}}{1-\tan\frac{3\pi}{4}\tan\frac{\pi}{6}}=\frac{(-1)+\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}}{1-(-1)\cdot\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}}=\frac{-1+\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}}{1+\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}}=\frac{\frac{-3+\sqrt{3}}{3}}{\frac{3+\sqrt{3}}{3}}=\frac{-3+\sqrt{3}}{3+\sqrt{3}} \end{equation*}

\begin{align*} \sin\theta\amp =\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right) \amp \cos\theta\amp =\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right)\\ \tan\theta\amp =\cot\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right) \amp \cot\theta\amp =\tan\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right)\\ \sec\theta\amp =\csc\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right) \amp \csc\theta\amp =\sec\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\theta\right) \end{align*}

\end{mybox}

Example 3.2.7.

Prove the Sum Formula for Sine

\begin{equation*} \sin(\alpha+\beta)=\sin\alpha\cos\beta+\cos\alpha\sin\beta \end{equation*}
Solution.

We begin by using the cofunction identity

\begin{align*} \sin(\alpha+\beta)\amp =\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-(\alpha+\beta)\right)\amp \amp \mbox{Cofunction Identity}\\ \amp =\cos\left(\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\alpha\right)-\beta\right)\\ \amp =\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\alpha\right)\cos\beta+\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2}-\alpha\right)\sin\beta\amp \amp \mbox{Difference Formula for Cosine}\\ \amp =\sin\alpha\cos\beta+\sin\alpha\sin\beta\amp \amp \mbox{Cofunction Identity}\\ \amp =\sin\alpha\cos\beta+\cos\alpha\sin\beta \end{align*}
