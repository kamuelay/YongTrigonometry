Proof

We add the sum and difference formula for cosine:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcl} \cos(\alpha+\beta)\amp =\amp \cos\alpha\cos\beta-\sin\alpha\sin\beta\\ +\cos(\alpha-\beta)\amp =\amp \cos\alpha\cos\beta+\sin\alpha\sin\beta\\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}

Dividing both sides by 2, we get

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{2}[\cos(\alpha+\beta)+\cos(\alpha-\beta)]=\cos\alpha\cos\beta \end{equation*}

We add the sum and difference formula for sine:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcl} \sin(\alpha+\beta)\amp =\amp \sin\alpha\cos\beta+\cos\alpha\sin\beta\\ +\sin(\alpha-\beta)\amp =\amp \sin\alpha\cos\beta-\cos\alpha\sin\beta\\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}

Dividing both sides by 2, we get

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{2}[\sin(\alpha+\beta)+\sin(\alpha-\beta)=\sin\alpha\cos\beta \end{equation*}

We subtract the sum and difference formula for sine:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcl} \sin(\alpha+\beta)\amp =\amp \sin\alpha\cos\beta+\cos\alpha\sin\beta\\ -\sin(\alpha-\beta)\amp =\amp \sin\alpha\cos\beta-\cos\alpha\sin\beta\\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}

Dividing both sides by 2, we get

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{2}[\sin(\alpha+\beta)-\sin(\alpha-\beta)=\sin\beta\cos\alpha \end{equation*}

We subtract the difference and sum formula for cosine:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcl} \cos(\alpha-\beta)\amp =\amp \cos\alpha\cos\beta+\sin\alpha\sin\beta\\ -\cos(\alpha+\beta)\amp =\amp \cos\alpha\cos\beta-\sin\alpha\sin\beta\\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}

Dividing both sides by 2, we get

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{2}[\cos(\alpha-\beta)-\cos(\alpha+\beta)]=\sin\alpha\sin\beta \end{equation*}
