Proof

We first let \(\alpha=\frac{u+v}{2}\) and \(\beta=\frac{u-v}{2}\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} \alpha+\beta=\frac{u+v}{2}+\frac{u-v}{2}=\frac{2u}{2}=u \end{equation*}

and

\begin{equation*} \alpha-\beta=\frac{u+v}{2}-\frac{u-v}{2}=\frac{2v}{2}=v \end{equation*}

Substituting these values for \(\alpha\text{,}\) \(\beta\text{,}\) \(\alpha+\beta\text{,}\) and \(\alpha-\beta\) into the Product-to-Sum Formulas, we get

\begin{align*} \sin\left(\frac{u+v}{2}\right)\cos\left(\frac{u-v}{2}\right)\amp =\amp \frac{1}{2}[\sin(u)+\sin(v)]\\ \cos\left(\frac{u+v}{2}\right)\sin\left(\frac{u-v}{2}\right)\amp =\amp \frac{1}{2}[\sin(u)-\sin(v)]\\ \cos\left(\frac{u+v}{2}\right)\cos\left(\frac{u-v}{2}\right)\amp =\amp \frac{1}{2}[\cos(u)+\cos(v)]\\ \sin\left(\frac{u+v}{2}\right)\sin\left(\frac{u-v}{2}\right)\amp =\amp \frac{1}{2}[\cos(v)-\cos(u)] \end{align*}

Multiplying both sides by 2 and replacing \(u\) with \(\alpha\) and \(v\) with \(\beta\) we end up with the Sum-to-Product Formula. (multiply the last equation by -1)
