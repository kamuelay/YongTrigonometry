The radian measure of a central angle in a circle is the ratio of the length of the arc on a circle subtended by the angle to the radius. If \(r\) is the radius of the circle, \(\theta\) is the angle, and \(s\) is the arc length, then we have the following

\begin{equation*} \theta=\frac{s}{r} \end{equation*}
