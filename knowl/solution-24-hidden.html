Since \(\sin\theta\lt 0\) and \(\cos\theta\lt 0\text{,}\) we refer to Table 1.5.2 and see that \(\theta\) is in Quadrant III. From Table 1.5.2 we know \(\tan\theta>0\text{,}\) \(\csc\theta\lt 0\text{,}\) \(\sec\theta\lt 0\text{,}\) \(\cot\theta>0\text{.}\) From the quotient identity, we have

\begin{equation*} \tan\theta=\frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}=\frac{\frac{12}{13}}{\frac{5}{13}}=\frac{12}{5} \end{equation*}

Next, using the reciprocal identities we get

\begin{align*} \csc\theta\amp =\frac{1}{\sin\theta}=\frac{1}{-\frac{12}{13}}=-\frac{13}{12},\amp \sec\theta\amp =\frac{1}{\cos\theta}=\frac{1}{-\frac{5}{13}}=-\frac{13}{5},\amp \cot\theta\amp =\frac{1}{\tan\theta}=\frac{1}{\frac{12}{5}}=\frac{5}{12} \end{align*}
