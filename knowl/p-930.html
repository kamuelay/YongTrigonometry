\begin{mybox}[Properties of the Dot Product] Let \(\mathbf{u}\text{,}\) \(\mathbf{v}\text{,}\) and \(\mathbf{w}\) be vectors and let \(c\) be a scalar (real number). Then

  1. \(\displaystyle \mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v}=\mathbf{v}\cdot\mathbf{u}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle c(\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v})=(c\mathbf{u})\cdot\mathbf{v}=\mathbf{u}\cdot(c\mathbf{v})\)

  3. \(\displaystyle (\mathbf{u}+\mathbf{v})\cdot\mathbf{w}=\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{w}+\mathbf{v}\cdot\mathbf{w}\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{u}=\|\mathbf{u}\|^2\)

  5. \(\displaystyle \mathbf{0}\cdot\mathbf{v}=0\)
