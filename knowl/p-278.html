\Question In 2013–2017, Hōkūleʻa circumnavigated the globe with a voyage mission to mālama honua - “care for Island Earth” and all people and places as `ohana ( “family”). The 40,000 nautical mile voyage stopped at more than 150 ports in 18 nations. Throughout this voyage, Earth rotates on an axis that runs between the North Pole and the South Pole. Every point on the Earth experiences this rotation through an angular speed and linear velocity (See Section 1.2.5). If we assume the Earth completes one rotation in 24 hours and that the Earth is a perfect sphere, with a radius of \(R=4,000\) miles, then for each of the following ports of the Mālama Honua voyage and it's given latitude, \(\phi\text{,}\) calculate the following

  1. \(r\text{,}\) the distance from the port to the Earth's Axis of Rotation (in miles, rounded to 1 decimal);

  2. \(\omega\text{,}\) the angular velocity (in radians per hour, rounded to 4 decimals)

  3. \(v\text{,}\) the linear speed (in miles per hour, rounded to the nearest whole number)
