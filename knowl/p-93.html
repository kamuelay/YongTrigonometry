\end{mybox} Notice the ratio \(\displaystyle\frac{\theta}{360^{\circ}}\) is the proportion of the angle \(\theta\) (in degrees) to one complete circle. Additionally, the circumference of a circle is given by \(2\pi r\text{.}\) Therefore, the arc length is simply the proportion of the central angle to the whole circle multiplied by the circumference of the circle.

\begin{equation*} s=\mbox{arc length} =\mbox{(proportion of circle)} \cdot\mbox{(circumference)} =\left(\frac{\theta}{360^{\circ}}\right)\cdot(2\pi r) \end{equation*}
