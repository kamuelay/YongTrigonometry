Solution 1.3.2.1.

The point \(P(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2},-\frac{1}{2})\) gives us \(x=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\) and \(y=-\frac{1}{2}\text{.}\) Then we have

\begin{align*} \sin \theta\amp =y=-\frac{1}{2} \amp \cos \theta\amp =x=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \amp \tan \theta\amp =\frac{y}{x}=\frac{-\frac{1}{2}}{\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}}=-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}\\ \csc \theta\amp =\frac{1}{y}=\frac{1}{\frac{1}{2}}=-2 \amp \sec \theta\amp =\frac{1}{x}=\frac{1}{\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}}=\frac{2\sqrt{3}}{3} \amp \cot \theta\amp =\frac{x}{y}=\frac{=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}}{-\frac{1}{2}}=-\sqrt{3} \end{align*}
