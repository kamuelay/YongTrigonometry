We make sure our calculator is in degree or radian mode. For simplicity, we will round our answers to four decimals.

  1. Input: SIN(22); Output: \(0.3746\)

  2. Input: COS(5); Output: \(0.9962\)

  3. Since most calculators do not have a key for cotangent, we Input: (1/TAN(53)); Output: \(\dfrac{1}{1.3270}\approx0.7536\)

  4. Since this problem uses radians, we must change the mode on our calculator then Input: COS(5); Output: \(0.2837\text{.}\)

Notice that \(\cos5^{\circ}\neq\cos5\mbox{ rad}\text{.}\) This is why it is important to double check if your calculator is on degree or radian mode.
