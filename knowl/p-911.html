Another example of tacking is when Hokulea is sailing to Rapa Nui. When it is When Hokulea is about 300 miles west of Rapa Nui , the navigators will begin a series of close tacks between \(25^{\circ}\) S and \(29^{\circ}\) S to search for the island; they will have to pass within about 30 miles of the island in daylight to see it from the canoe.
