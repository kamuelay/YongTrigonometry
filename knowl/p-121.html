\Question At the start of this section you learned that the latitude of a place is the angle between a line drawn from the center of the earth to that point and the equatorial plane. If the radius of the earth is 3,959 miles, calculate the arc length, \(s\text{,}\) along the surface of the earth for \begin{tasks}(2) \task \(1^{\circ}\) of latitude (in miles, rounded to 2 decimals) \task \(1'\) of latitude (in miles, rounded to 2 decimals). \task \(1''\) of latitude (in feet, rounded to the nearest integer) \end{tasks} A nautical mile, frequently used in navigation, is slightly longer than a mile on land. One nautical mile was historically defined to be the arc length corresponding to one minute of latitude. Check your answer in (b) with the value of one nautical mile.
