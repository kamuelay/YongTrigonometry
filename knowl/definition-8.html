Definition 1.2.24. Linear Speed.

Suppose an object moves along a circle with radius \(r\) and \(\theta\) (measured in radians) is the angle transversed in time \(t\text{.}\) Let \(s\) be the distance the object traveled in time \(t\text{.}\) Then the linear speed, \(v\text{,}\) of the object is given by

\begin{equation*} v=\frac{s}{t} \end{equation*}
