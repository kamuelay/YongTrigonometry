One method people use to identify their position is by looking at the latitude. These imaginary lines that form circles around the Earth run parallel to the Equator. The latitude of a place is defined as the angle between a line drawn from the center of the earth to that point and the equatorial plane. For any point in the Northern Hemisphere, a navigator can measure their latitude by determining the angle Hōkūpaʻa (North Star) makes with the horizon.
