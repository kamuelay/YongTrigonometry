Note that \(\frac{5\pi}{7}\) is not in the interval \(\left[-\frac{\pi}{2},\frac{\pi}{2}\right]\text{.}\) In order to evaluate the expression, we first need to find an angle, \(\theta\text{,}\) such that \(-\frac{\pi}{2}\leq\theta\leq\frac{\pi}{2}\) and \(\sin\frac{5\pi}{7}=\sin\theta\text{.}\) Since \(\frac{\pi}{2}\lt \frac{5\pi}{7}\lt \pi\) means \(\frac{5\pi}{7}\) is in Quadrant II. Recall from [cross-reference to target(s) "sec_unit-circle" missing or not unique] that our reference angle is \(\theta=\pi-\frac{5\pi}{7}=\frac{2\pi}{7}\text{.}\)

Since \(\frac{2\pi}{7}\) is in the interval \(\left[-\frac{\pi}{2},\frac{\pi}{2}\right]\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \sin^{-1}\left(\sin\frac{5\pi}{7}\right)=\sin^{-1}\left(\sin\frac{2\pi}{7}\right)=\frac{2\pi}{7} \end{equation*}
