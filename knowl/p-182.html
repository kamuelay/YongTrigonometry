For an angle \(\theta\) in standard position, let \(P(x,y)\) be the point on the terminal side of \(\theta\) that is also on the circle \(x^2+y^2=r^2\text{.}\) Then

\begin{align*} \sin \theta\amp =\frac{y}{r} \amp \cos \theta\amp =\frac{x}{r} \amp \tan \theta\amp =\frac{y}{x} (x\neq0)\\ \csc \theta\amp =\frac{r}{y} (y\neq0) \amp \sec \theta\amp =\frac{r}{x} (x\neq0) \amp \cot \theta\amp =\frac{x}{y} (y\neq0) \end{align*}
