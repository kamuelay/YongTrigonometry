Since this is a right triangle, we know one angle is \(90^{\circ}\text{,}\) we also know that the other angle is \(50^{\circ}\) and the adjacent side is 16. To solve this triangle, we will need to determine the values of \(a\text{,}\) \(c\text{,}\) and \(\beta\text{.}\) We begin by finding \(\beta\text{.}\) Since \(50^{\circ}+\beta=90^{\circ}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \beta=90^{\circ}-50^{\circ}=40^{\circ} \end{equation*}

Next, we will solve for \(a\text{.}\) If we use the angle \(50^{\circ}\text{,}\) the adjacent side is 16 and \(a\) is the opposite side to the angle. The trigonometric function that relates these is tangent:

\begin{equation*} \tan50^{\circ}=\frac{a}{16} \end{equation*}

multiplying both sides by 16 we get

\begin{equation*} a=16\cdot\tan50^{\circ}\approx19.07 \end{equation*}

Using the Pythagorean Theorem, we get

\begin{equation*} c^2=16^2+19.07^2\approx619.66 \end{equation*}

Thus

\begin{equation*} c\approx\sqrt{619.66}\approx24.89 \end{equation*}
