\Question The oeoe, or Hawaiian bullroarer, is made by drilling holes into a kamani seed or coconut shell, then threading a long string through the holes to securing it. When the oeoe is swung by the string, a whistling sound is produced, similar to the sound of the wind on the top of mountains. If a girl is swinging an oeoe at the end of 3 foot long rope at a rate of 180 revolutions per minute, calculate 1) the angular speed measured in radians per minute; 2) the linear speed of the shell in miles per hour (round to two decimal places).
