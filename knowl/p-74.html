\begin{mybox}[Relationships Between Degrees and Radians] If a circle with radius 1 is drawn, there are \(360^{\circ}\) and the full arc length is the circumference, which is \(2\pi\text{.}\) Therefore, the relationship between degrees and radians is:

\begin{equation*} 360^{\circ}=2\pi \mbox{ radian} , \mbox{or } 180^{\circ}=\pi\mbox{ radian} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 1\mbox{ radian} =\frac{180^{\circ}}{\pi} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 1^{\circ}=\frac{\pi}{180}\mbox{ radian} \end{equation*}

  1. To convert degree to radians, multiply by \(\dfrac{2\pi\mbox{ radians} }{360^{\circ}}\) or \(\dfrac{\pi\mbox{ radian} }{180^{\circ}}\)

  2. To convert radians to degrees, multiply by \(\dfrac{360^{\circ}}{2\pi\mbox{ radians} }\) or \(\dfrac{180^{\circ}}{\pi\mbox{ radian} }\)
