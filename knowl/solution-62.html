Solution 4.3.26.1.

First we note that since the velocity is 10 knots, we have \(\|\mathbf{v}\|=10\text{.}\) Next we will need to find the direction. Since \(\theta\) is measured from the positive \(x\)-axis, we see that

\begin{equation*} \theta=90^{\circ}+22.5^{\circ}=112.5^{\circ} \end{equation*}

To find the vector, will need the horizontal and vertical components.

\begin{align*} v_x\amp =\amp \|\mathbf{v}\|\cos\theta=10\cos112.5^{\circ}\approx-3.8\\ v_y\amp =\amp \|\mathbf{v}\|\sin\theta=10\sin112.5^{\circ}\approx9.2 \end{align*}

Thus

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{v}\approx-3.8\mathbf{i}+9.2\mathbf{j}=\langle-3.8,9.2\rangle \end{equation*}
